Ashesi Experiential Transcript - Pillars, Elements & Metrics

Pillar 1: Scholarship

Scholarship is defined as demonstration of high-quality knowledge and/or academic achievement in a particular field or various fields, obtained from detailed study, critical thinking, rigorous interaction with different materials and perspectives and analytical reasoning, along with a demonstrated passion in and beyond the classroom. Scholarship has three elements: Academic Achievements, Research & Discovery and Professionalism.

  • 1a) Academic Achievements: excellent performance in coursework or self-directed work in an academic discipline.

    • Metrics

    *Being on the Deanâ€™s List

    *Winning an academic competition

    *Demonstrating knowledge of subject area in context outside of the classroom

    *Other

  • 1b) Research & Discovery: detailed academic/self-directed investigation into a topic sparked by knowledge gap and curiosity, interest and the desire to make a difference.

    • Metrics

    *Undergraduate research/experimentation/project that synthesizes information across a variety of disciplines or advances a new thought in a chosen field

    *Self-initiated research/experimentation/project in unexplored/chosen fields that contributes or has the potential to contribute to the advancement of the field and/ or synthesizes information across a variety of disciplines

    *Presenting at a conference or sharing knowledge or skill publicly

    *Other

  • 1c) Professionalism: a sense of responsibility and ownership towards academic work; commitment to quality; being well informed about a chosen field; good knowledge of professional standards in a chosen field demonstrated by, but not limited to, discipline-specific writing skills and academic integrity.

    • Metrics

    *Undergraduate research/experimentation/project that synthesizes information across a variety of disciplines or advances a new thought in a chosen field

    *Self-initiated research/experimentation/project in unexplored/chosen fields that contributes or has the potential to contribute to the advancement of the field and/ or synthesizes information across a variety of disciplines

    *Presenting at a conference or sharing knowledge or skill publicly

    *Other

    Pillar 2: Leadership

    Leadership is defined as moving others to act responsibly or inspiring them to rise to their best; initiating and driving positive change through ethical, innovative acts; providing others with fair and equitable opportunities to attain their full potential; self-leadership. Leadership has three elements: Problem-solving, Grit & Courage and Professionalism.

  • 2a) Problem-solving: employing creative well-thought through solutions in response to pressing needs.

    • Metrics

    *The ability to define and solve problems using critical thinking approaches in leadership

    *The launch or active support of an initiative or project that employs creative problem-solving on or outside campus

    *Initiating and driving positive social change among others (peers/older people) on or outside campus

    *Acknowledging inequities and working effectively with diverse groups of people to solve unique problems creatively and innovatively

    *Other

  • 2b) Grit & Courage: resilience, passion, stamina, self-discipline, persistence and perseverance; single-minded pursuit of goals; maintaining consistent long-term energy and enthusiasm towards an enterprise in spite of perceived or actual setbacks; having a growth mindset; daring to enter unfamiliar territory, to birth new ideas, to break glass ceilings, to break the mold; making quantum leaps in innovation; taking promising strides in entrepreneurship.

    • Metrics

    *Successfully overcoming a major setback in leadership

    *Completing a long-term project with excellence

    *Successfully challenging the status quo in pursuit of laudable goals

    *Successfully establishing or supporting the establishment of a business venture or coming up with an innovation

    *Successfully engaging with difference and exploring the impact of diversity in shaping perspectives and approaches

    *Other

  • 2c) Professionalism: demonstrating good work ethic, discretion, excellent recognition of when to appropriately apply knowledge of subject area, vision and clear communication, and treating others equitably and with respect in all leadership interactions.

    • Metrics

    *Self-development in good leadership evidenced by participation in conferences, workshops, webinars and in-person or online courses, leading a workshop e.g. career reflection workshop

    *Official recognition as a leader during a career experiential learning experience such as an internship

    *Selected as a Career Peer Advisor

    *Other

    Pillar 3: Citizenship

    Citizenship is defined as demonstrated interest and concern about communities, organizations, nations and continents (especially the African continent); productive involvement in communal affairs; tolerance and sensitivity towards differences in communities; a sense of responsibility and ownership towards assigned work; strong work ethic; consistent self-improvement in a chosen field; a general sense of workplace appropriateness. Citizenship has three elements: Service, Engagement & Impact, Cultural Sensibility & Adaptability and Professionalism.

  • 3a) Service, Engagement & Impact: offering aid to others in need; committed productive involvement in communal affairs; visible, measurable positive effects or potential for same on society.

    • Metrics

    *Acts of service in communities or advocacy in respect of problems in unserved communities that are sorely in need of solutions

    *Participating and excelling in competitions which focus on community development, creative-problem solving or strategic planning within the Ashesi community, a region or a nation

    *Representing, modelling and demonstrating Ashesi core values or learning goals at programs or events

    *Undergraduate research findings/patent-worthy innovations that have the potential to make positive impact on society

    *Other

  • 3b) Cross/Inter Cultural Sensibility & Adaptability: the ability to codeswitch both in speech and behaviour and be respectful in different contexts and settings; the ability to adapt to different settings and be culturally conscious and intelligent, responsive and appropriate; knowing how to relate to both people with African and other worldviews with respect; being committed and open to collaborating with diverse people.

    • Metrics

    *Successful participation in a school exchange program

    *Effectively working on an initiative/project/campaign in a multi-cultural/ multi-generational team; evidence of successful interactions with international students on campus and beyond

    *Successfully complete a global engagement (internship, conference, study abroad) relevant to area of study or a project of interest

    *Successfully completing internship with a multinational company

    *Other

  • 3c) Professionalism: displaying integrity and strong work ethic in all dealings as an active citizen of the workplace community.

    • Metrics

    *Successfully completing Mentorship/Job Shadowing/Internship Programme/Exit Strategy Programme with Career Services

    *Self-development in professional standards of a chosen career evidenced by attending conferences, webinars etc.

    *Self-development in cultural sensibility and adaptability evidenced by attending conferences workshops, at least 3 career services sessions within the year, webinars and completing online courses on the subject, and/or leading/facilitating conversations on specific topics related to sensitivity, diversity, equity, and inclusion.

    *Other
